I am a mathematician specializing in machine learning and data science. I earned my Ph.D. in Mathematics from the Instituto de Matemática Pura e Aplicada (IMPA) in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. My thesis focused on boosting and concentration of measure methods in machine learning. I completed my M.Sc. in Mathematics at the Universidade de São Paulo (ICMC-USP), with a dissertation on ergodic theory in homogeneous flows and Ratner's theorems, and I hold a Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from the same institution.

Currently, I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at ICMC-USP in São Carlos, Brazil, funded by FAPESP. My research involves using satellite imagery, spatial metrics, and neural networks to map urban segments and analyze crime types, aiming to understand the socioeconomic and cultural influences on criminal dynamics. In 2023, I held a postdoctoral position at IMPA, funded by FAPERJ, where I led machine learning research projects and authored several scientific papers.

In 2023, I worked as a Data Scientist Specialist at Samsung Research Brazil, where I developed AI solutions for healthcare initiatives, leveraging time-series data and sensor information to enhance user experiences and well-being.

I have also worked as an AI consultant for various high-profile companies in partnership with IMPA during my time at the Center for Project and Innovation (Centro de Projeto e Inovação - PI). These clients include the Rio de Janeiro City Hall, Cartesi Blockchain, Rede Globo, Grupo Dasa, and Stone Pagamentos. These projects spans diverse applications, such as rainfall and flood predictions, blockchain lottery systems, keyword extraction for media recommendations, MRI image analysis for pregnancy-related issues, and credit decision enhancements for financial services.
