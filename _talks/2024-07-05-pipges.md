As datasets become larger, the task of merging entries from different databases becomes increasingly costly in modern Machine Learning pipelines. To reduce the cost of comparing entries, the efficient creation of blocks containing similar items is essential as a preprocessing step. In this presentation, we will explore a boosting-based method that generates binary codes to capture the similarity between the data, facilitating the formation of these blocks. In addition to a computational perspective on the method, we will also examine what types of mathematical tools can be used to justify its practical application.
