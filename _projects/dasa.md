The amniotic fluid detection algorithm was developed by the Institute of Pure and Applied Mathematics (IMPA) in collaboration with healthcare professionals from the Dasa group. Its purpose is to identify and quantify the presence of amniotic fluid in medical images, specifically in the context of fetal development during pregnancy.

The algorithm utilizes advanced image processing techniques and machine learning algorithms to analyze 3D magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans. These scans provide a comprehensive view of the fetus and the surrounding amniotic fluid. By training the algorithm on a dataset of 700 3D MRI images, it learns to accurately identify and segment regions containing amniotic fluid.

One of the key objectives of this algorithm is to measure the volume and dynamics of the amniotic fluid, as it directly correlates with the well-being and development of the fetus. Deviations from normal fluid levels can indicate potential pathologies or abnormalities in the baby. For example, the algorithm can detect esophageal obstruction by observing an increase in amniotic fluid volume due to the fetus being unable to swallow the fluid.

The algorithm's implementation involves a combination of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and artificial neural networks. CNNs simulate the basic functioning of the human brain by using artificial neurons and synapses to process and analyze image data. Through a process of iterative learning, the algorithm becomes proficient in recognizing and distinguishing amniotic fluid from other structures within the medical images.

The research team led by Dr. Paulo Orenstein, a researcher at IMPA, employed a feedback mechanism to enhance the algorithm's accuracy. By providing annotations and corrections based on expert analysis, the algorithm adapts and refines its detection capabilities. For instance, if the algorithm misidentifies a cyst as amniotic fluid, the error is flagged and used as a teaching signal to improve future interpretations.

The future vision for this research is to expand the algorithm's capabilities to analyze other fetal organs, such as the brain. However, this would require the availability of different datasets that focus on specific organ characteristics and abnormalities.

The development of the amniotic fluid detection algorithm falls under the purview of the IMPA's Center for Projects and Innovation (Centro Pi). The center aims to foster collaborations between the institute and public and private entities to advance the application of mathematics in various domains.
