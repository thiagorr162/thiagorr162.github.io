We aimed at developing a machine learning model to make rainfall prediction in the city of Rio de Janeiro. The project was a collaborative effort between IMPA, the municipal government, and Google, with the objective of enhancing the accuracy and timeliness of rainfall forecasts using state-of-the-art artificial intelligence techniques.

During the project, we had access to comprehensive rainfall data provided by the municipal government. We worked tirelessly to integrate this data with information obtained from various sources, including rain gauges, radar systems, and satellite imagery.

Our objective was to design and train a sophisticated algorithm capable of predicting the occurrence and intensity of rainfall. By leveraging the diverse and multifaceted data sets, our algorithm aimed to empower the municipal government with accurate and timely information, enabling them to optimize planning processes and implement proactive measures to prevent potential environmental catastrophes.
